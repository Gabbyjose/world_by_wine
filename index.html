Loire Valley
Grape of Fame:
Cabernet Franc
Other Grapes:
Melon de Bourgogne, Gamay, and Pinot noir
Sub-Regions:
Anjou, Nantes, Saumur, Touraine, Centre
If You Like:
Light like a Pinot Noir, but with more of a vegetable aftertaste, try this red to pair with a fish or pasta dish.
Things to Say:
"You can really taste the earth in this musty Cab Franc, unlike its cousin the Cabernet Sauvignon"
Alsace
Grape of Fame:
Reisling
Other Grapes:
Pinot Blanc, Pinot Gris, Gewurztraminer and Crémant d’Alsace
Sub-Regions:
Bas-Rhin and Haut-Rhin
If You Like:
If you're a Reisling fan, you should venture to taste its brother across the border. Typically a German grape, Alsace has an interesting history that straddles German and French winemaking tradition, giving rise to a new kind of Reisling.
Things to Say:
"Unlike the German Reisling, the Reisling here in Alsace are nice and dry."
Bordeaux
Grape of Fame:
Cabernet Sauvignon
Other Grapes:
Merlot, Petit Verdot, Cabernet Franc, Malbec,
Primary Flavors:
Black Currant, Plum, Graphite, Cedar, Violet
Sub-Regions:
Medoc, Graves, Linournais, Entre-Deux-Mers
If You Like:
Bordeaux's natural rival grows across the ocean in the hills of Napa Valley. The cabernet sauvignon grape from California has a similar body and strong fruit. Try these side by side and see if you can tell the difference.
Things to Say:
"I prefer a well aged Bordeaux over a Napa Cab any day because I love the oak flavor."
Rhone Valley
Grape of Fame:
Syrah (Northern Rhone) and Grenache (Southern Rhone)
Other Grapes:
Mourverde, Cinsault, Carignan, and Petite Sirah
Primary Flavors:
Berry fruit on the palate, with notes of coffee, spice, and game
Sub-Regions:
Cote-Rotie, Condrieu, Chateau-Grillet, Saint-Joseph, Crozes-Hermitage, Hermitage, Cornas, Saint-Peray, Cotes du Vivarais, Cotes du Rhone, Coteaux du Tricastin, Vacqueyras, Resteau, Cairanne, Gigondas, Vinsobres, Lirac
If You Like:
The Rhone valley is one of France's most varied. Loves of smoke and spice will find their home here among the beautiful blends of Syrah and Grenache grapes. Spanish wine lovers should also come here.
Things to Say:
"I can't wait to have the opportunity to try another Hermitage, because we all know that's some of the rarest of the Rhone wines."
Burgundy
Grape of Fame:
Pinot Noir
Other Grapes:
Gamay
Primary Flavors:
Full bodied with flavors of the earth
Sub-Regions:
Beaujolais, Cote de Nuits, Cote de Beaune, Cote Chalonnaise, Maconnais
If You Like:
Any fan of Pinot Noir has to try a Burgundy. Whether you drink your Pinots from the mega producers in California to the mom-and-pop winery in Australia, there is nothing like a Pinot Noir from Burgundy.
Things to Say:
"New World Pinot Noirs have nothing on the OG Burgundy, especially those old vine Burgundy's."
